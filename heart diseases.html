Heart Disease – Know the Facts

Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death worldwide

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease (also called cardiovascular disease) refers to several types of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. The most common type is coronary artery disease (CAD), where plaque builds up in the arteries that supply blood to the heart.

According to major health organizations, heart disease causes ≈ 1 in 5 deaths in many countries.

Common Types

  • Coronary artery disease (narrowed heart arteries)
  • Heart attack (myocardial infarction)
  • Heart failure
  • Arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats)
  • Heart valve disease
  • Congenital heart defects
  • Cardiomyopathy (diseased heart muscle)

Major Risk Factors

  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • High cholesterol
  • Smoking / tobacco use
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity / overweight
  • Physical inactivity
  • Unhealthy diet (high in salt, sugar, saturated fats)
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Age (risk increases after 45 in men, 55 in women)
  • Chronic stress

Warning Signs & Symptoms

Symptoms can vary – some people have no symptoms until a serious event occurs.

  • Chest pain or discomfort (pressure, squeezing, fullness)
  • Pain spreading to arms, neck, jaw, back or stomach
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue / unusual tiredness
  • Light-headedness, dizziness, fainting
  • Nausea, cold sweat
  • Heart palpitations (feeling your heart racing or pounding)

Call emergency services immediately if you suspect a heart attack – every minute counts!

Prevention – Small Changes Save Lives

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet (more fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins; less salt & processed food)
  • Be physically active – aim for at least 150 minutes moderate activity per week
  • Quit smoking and avoid second-hand smoke
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Control blood pressure, cholesterol and diabetes
  • Limit alcohol
  • Manage stress (meditation, yoga, good sleep)
  • Get regular medical check-ups
